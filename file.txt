In the 18th and 19th centuries, England became a dominant colonial power, with the establishment of colonies in North America, Africa, and Asia. The Industrial Revolution saw significant advancements in technology and industry, leading to the rise of the middle class and the growth of cities.

The 20th century saw England fight in two world wars, with significant contributions to the Allied victory. The post-war era saw the establishment of the National Health Service and the growth of the welfare state.

In recent years, England has faced significant political and social changes, including the rise of nationalist and populist movements and the Brexit referendum, which resulted in the UK's exit from the European Union.

Overall, the history of England is complex and diverse, marked by significant cultural, social, and political changes over the centuries.
